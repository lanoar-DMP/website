---

Consumer Price Index data still sits at the center of macro positioning because it shapes the market's path for policy, real income, and terminal rate expectations all at once.

When CPI cools faster than expected, duration typically catches a bid, breakevens can reset lower, and the market starts pricing a shallower policy path. When CPI re-accelerates, the opposite usually happens: front-end rates lift, growth assets reprice, and the macro narrative swings back toward policy restraint.

For a terminal product, CPI earns a permanent slot because it is both a top-down signal and a positioning trigger. It is one of the few releases that can reprice equities, rates, FX, and political sentiment inside a single session.

The practical takeaway is simple: if the dashboard only had room for one macro tile, CPI would still be the default. It is slow-moving as a data series, but extremely fast-moving as a catalyst.
